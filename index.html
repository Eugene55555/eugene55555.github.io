The Standard

What the Regulation Actually Says

27 CFR § 478.125 — Records of receipt and disposition

"Each licensee shall record the receipt and disposition of each firearm... in a bound record... [with] the date of receipt... the name and address of the person... the firearm's serial number... and the disposition information."

Questions

Frequently Asked

Do FFL holders have to keep acquisition and disposition records?

Yes. Federal law requires licensed firearms dealers to maintain acquisition and disposition records. The requirement is set out in 27 CFR § 478.125.

What does 27 CFR § 478.125 require?

It requires dealers to record, for each firearm acquired and disposed of, the date, name and address of the person, and firearm/serial details in a bound format that is not loose-leaf. This log book is pre-formatted to that layout.

Is this book "ATF approved"?

No log book is individually "approved" by ATF. This book is formatted to match the record layout inspectors look for under 27 CFR § 478.125. It is a compliance tool, not legal advice.

Paperback or Hardcover — which should I get?

Paperback ($13.99) is the everyday write-in log. Hardcover ($20.99) is the more durable, archival option for permanent records. Many dealers keep a hardcover master and a paperback working copy.

How many pages is the book?

120 pages, pre-formatted for acquisition and disposition entries with space for dates, names, addresses, serial numbers, and notes.

Who is it for?

Federal firearms licensees (FFLs), gunsmiths, manufacturers, and anyone required to maintain A&D records under 27 CFR Part 478.
